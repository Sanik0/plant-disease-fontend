Bacterial spot
Creates raised, scabby lesions on fruits and small, angular leaf spots with yellow halos. The bacteria spread rapidly during rainstorms and overhead irrigation.
February 25, 2025
Furasium wilt
Fusarium Wilt is a devastating soil-borne disease caused by the fungus Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. lycopersici that affects tomato plants worldwide. This pathogen invades the plant's vascular system through its roots, clogging the xylem vessels and preventing water and nutrient transport. Early symptoms include yellowing and wilting of lower leaves, often affecting one side of the plant first. As the disease progresses, plants exhibit stunted growth, leaf drop, and eventual collapse.
February 15, 2025
Early blight
Small, water-soaked lesions that enlarge into brown or black spots. Yellow halos often surround the spots. Lesions may dry and tear, giving a "shot-hole" appearance. Affects pepper and tomato plants primarily.
February 13, 2025
Late blight
Dark brown to black lesions on leaves and stems. Leaf lesions often start at tips or edges and expand rapidly. White mold (sporangia) may appear under leaves in moist conditions. Infected tissue becomes soft, water-soaked, and dies quickly. Can infect tubers, causing brownish rot under the skin.
Janury 20, 2025
Cercospora meloganae
Cercospora is a genus of fungi known for causing leaf spot diseases on a wide variety of plants. These fungi are plant pathogens, meaning they infect and damage plants, often leading to reduced growth or crop yield.
